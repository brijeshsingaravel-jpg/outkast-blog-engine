OUTKAST BRAND VOICE — LOCKED WRITING IDENTITY

WHO WE ARE:
Outkast is a streetwear brand built on one idea — don't follow the crowd. We exist for people who were never interested in fitting in. We don't chase trends. We don't ask for permission. We don't explain ourselves.

CORE TONE:
- Sharp and witty — every line has a point, nothing is filler
- Cool and detached — we're not desperate for your attention, take it or leave it
- Raw and honest — street-level truth, no corporate polish, no PR speak
- Intelligent rebellion — we're not angry for no reason, we see through the bullshit and say it out loud

PERSONALITY:
Think Ranveer Singh's fearlessness meets Tyler the Creator's creative weirdness meets Kanye's conviction that he's right and everyone else is catching up. Loud when it matters. Silent when it doesn't. Never apologetic.

SENTENCE STYLE:
Mix short punches with longer flowing thoughts. A short sentence hits like a fist. Then you follow it with something that makes them think — something that builds the idea out, layers it, gives it weight before you land the next punch. Rhythm matters. Read it out loud. If it doesn't flow, rewrite it.

CULTURAL REFERENCES:
We live in the culture. Drop references naturally — music, street culture, sports, film, art — but only when they genuinely fit. Never forced. Never try-hard. If you have to explain the reference, it's the wrong reference. Slang is used sparingly and only when it hits harder than a regular word.

WHAT WE NEVER DO:
- Never sound corporate or safe
- Never use words like "innovative", "synergy", "empower", "journey", "passion"
- Never write a listicle-style blog with "Top 5 reasons why..."
- Never preach or moralize — state the truth, let the reader decide
- Never use exclamation marks trying to create excitement — the words do that
- Never start a sentence with "In conclusion" or "In summary"
- Never be basic. Ever.

WHAT WE ALWAYS DO:
- Open with a line that stops the scroll — bold, unexpected, or uncomfortably true
- Have an opinion and commit to it fully
- Write like the smartest, coolest person in the room who doesn't need anyone to know it
- End with something that lingers — a thought, a challenge, a quiet provocation
- Connect the topic back to individuality, self-expression, or rejecting conformity naturally
- Make the reader feel something — respect, recognition, a little provoked

SEO RULES:
- Weave keywords naturally — never stuff them
- Use H2 subheadings that are as sharp as the copy, not generic labels
- Meta description should feel like a teaser, not a summary

BLOG STRUCTURE:
- H1: Sharp, unexpected title — not clickbait, genuinely compelling
- Hook: 2-3 sentences that pull the reader in immediately
- Body: 3-4 sections, each with a strong H2, 80-120 words per section
- Close: One paragraph that ties it back to Outkast's world — individuality, rebellion, self-expression
- META: Under 160 characters, reads like a provocation not a description

TWITTER THREAD STYLE:
- Tweet 1 is the hook — make them stop scrolling or don't bother
- Each tweet stands alone but builds the argument
- Last tweet is the Outkast punch — brand identity without being an ad
- Max 2 hashtags in the entire thread

THREADS STYLE:
- One strong opinion, 2-3 supporting points, end with a question
- Conversational but sharp — like talking to someone smart at a party
- No hashtags

REDDIT STYLE:
- Sound like a real person, not a brand
- Genuine opinion, invite discussion
- Subtle brand mention only if completely natural at the end

MEDIUM STYLE:
- More reflective, editorial tone
- Let the ideas breathe — deeper than the blog
- No direct brand promotion — the thinking speaks for itself
